⚠️ Program notes & calf guardrails
StrongFirst circuit
All three lifts are done in slow-circuit order: squat → press → pull. Complete all rungs of a ladder before starting the next ladder.
TRM weight is fixed
The weight stays the same for all 8 weeks. Volume (reps/ladders) increases, not intensity. Set your TRM in the Testing tab first.
Calf zero-load rule
Goblet squat to a box only — no full depth until pain-free. No ankle drive on press. Stop any set if calf tightens.
Swing days are easy
Swings on Tue/Fri are recovery — not a workout. 7 reps on the minute, 15 sets, maximally explosive but low fatigue.
Week 7 is deload
Volume drops significantly. Don't skip it — it sets up the week 8 test. Trust the process.
Week 8 is testing
Test your TRM again. Shoot for a PR vs. your baseline. Log your results in the Testing tab.
TRM Testing — Find Your Working Weight
For each movement pattern, enter how many reps you can perform with perfect technique at each KB weight. Your 8–10 TRM is the heaviest weight where you hit 8–10 clean reps before technical breakdown. This weight will be used across all 8 weeks.
⚠️ Do these tests on separate days with full rest. Test squat one day, press another, pull another. Do NOT rush — your TRM determines the entire program.
Once you save all three TRMs, the program will auto-fill your working weights and show a recommendation on every exercise. Return here after week 8 to retest and start a new cycle.
